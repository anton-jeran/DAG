NN-DAG: Real-time Neural-network-based diffuse acoustic generator

Authors: Anton Ratnarajah, Shi-Xiong Zhang, Meng Yu, Zhenyu Tang, Dinesh Manocha and Dong Yu
Abstract: We propose a neural-network-based diffuse acoustic generator (NN-DAG) for generating room impulse responses (RIRs) for a given acoustic environment. Our NN-DAG takes room dimension, listener and speaker positions, and reverberation time (RT60) as inputs and models specular and diffuse reflections for a given acoustic environment. The proposed NN-DAG is capable of simulating RIRs for a given input RT60 with an average error of 0.02s. We evaluate our approach using Google Speech API, Microsoft Speech API, and Kaldi tools. We show that our proposed NN-DAG with batch size 1 is 400 times faster than a state-of-the-art diffuse acoustic simulator (DAS) on CPU and gives a similar performance to DAS in automatic speech recognition (ASR) experiments. Our NN-DAG is 12 times faster than an existing GPU-based RIR simulator gpuRIR. We show that our NN-DAG outperforms gpuRIR by 2.5% in an AMI far-field ASR experiment.
